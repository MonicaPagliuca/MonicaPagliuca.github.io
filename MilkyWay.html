The Milky Way Galaxy

The Milky Way is the galaxy that contains our Solar System.

Overview

A galaxy is an extremely large group of stars, gas, and dust bound together by gravity. The Milky Way galaxy contains our Sun, the stars, and all the planets we know of. There are billions of other galaxies in the Universe, however there are only three that can be seen with a telescope.

Shape

The Milky Way has a spiral shape, just like more than two-thirds of the galaxies we know of. At the center of the spiral, there is a large amount of energy. Astronomers conclude that the center of the Milky Way is a supermassive black hole.

Formation

From a combination of mathematical models and observations, there have been theories developed of how the Universe began. A mathematical set of ideas that formed the basis of the Big Bang, include Albert Einstein's general theory of relativity, along with standard theories of fundamental particles.
