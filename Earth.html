Our Planet, Earth


Earth is the third planet from the Sun, and largest of the terrestrial planets

Orbit and Rotation

Earth is constantly orbiting the Sun. While it does this, it completes one full rotation every 23.9 hours, although it takes 365.25 days to make a full orbit around the Sun. Earth has 4 seasons, all of which occur because of Earth's axis of rotation, which is tilted 23.4 degrees.

Surface

Earth has volcanoes, valleys, and mountains - similar to Mars and Venus. The Earth's lithosphere is made up of a collection of very large plates that are always in movement. Earth's oceans make up 70% of it's surface, and almost all volcanoes are in the ocean.

Atmosphere

Earth's atmosphere consists of 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and 1% other gases. The atmosphere affects the weather and climate, and without it we would be exposed to the Sun's harmful radiation. It also helps protect us from objects coming from space, such as meteoroids.

Magnetosphere

Because of our planet's fast paced rotation and molten nickel-iron core, we have what is called a magnetic field. When charged particles from the solar wind get trapped there, they then collide with air molecules above the Earth's magnetic poles. An interesting fact, is that our magnetic field is what makes compass needles point toawrds the North Pole, no matter which way you turn.
